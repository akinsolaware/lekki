Discover Lekki Free Zone

The Zone, which situates across 16,500 hectares of land, is phased into four different Quadrants for ease of development namely; the South West Quadrant which is phase 1, covering about 3,000 hectares and currently being developed by the Lekki Free Zone Development Company.

The South East Quadrant is approximately 2,700 hectares and at present, being developed by the Dangote Group as a Petrochemical Hub which equally houses the Fertilizer plant while a part of the North West Quadrant is undergoing development by the North West Quadrant Development Company, and the North East Quadrant, which is still open to prospective investors. Also available for investment opportunity is over 2,000 hectares of land across the Northwest and Southwest Quadrants.

The Zone has in the pipeline, a dedicated Seaport, and Airport. The construction for the Seaport is ongoing, and the plan is to complete and commence operations in the last quarter of 2020.

Features

Unique Location

It is within the reach of domestic market of Nigeria with a population of over 200 million people. It also has easy access to the neighbouring countries in West Africa and central Africa with a population of 500 million people. It is within the easy reach of European and American market.

Regional Characteristics

Lekki Free Zone is about 50Km from Lagos city centre, 70 Km from the Murtala Mohammed Int’l Airport (MMIA) and 10 Km from the new Lekki Int'l Airport. It is also about 50 Km from Apapa port, West africa’s largest harbour.

The new Lekki deep sea port is located right inside the Lekki Free Zone and will be linked by rail and road network to all the commercial states of Nigeria and neighbouring countries.

Our Environment

LAGOS

Lagos State, located in the southwestern part of Nigeria, is an administrative division and the industrial capital city of the country. It is the most populous city in Nigeria and the second fastest-growing city in Africa. The population of Lagos is estimated at 21 million, making Lagos the largest city in Africa.

The indigenous people of Lagos state belong to the Yoruba ethnic group, and the languages spoken in Lagos are predominantly Yoruba and English. The population of Lagos, however, comprises of people from all over the country, thus making it a multi-ethnic city.

The state hosts the nation’s two largest seaports; Apapa and Tin Can Island, and has more than 2,000 industries of various sizes and descriptions.

Supporting Infrastructure

The Lekki Deep Seaport

The Lekki Deep Sea Port, the deepest in West Africa, is a multi-purpose, Deep Sea Port at the heart of the Lagos Free Trade Zone. Lekki Deep Sea Port will be one of the most modern ports in West Africa, offering enormous support to the burgeoning commercial operation across Lekki Free Zone, Lagos, Nigeria, and the entire West African region.

The Airport

Discussions are currently ongoing for the development of the proposed Airport, which will offer massive support for the expanding commercial activities in the Zone, Lagos, Nigeria, and West Africa.
