Lekki Free Zone Master Plan - The Quadrants

The zone is divided into 4 Quadrants. Each Quadrant is designed to accommodate a group of industries that has relative synergy so as to ensure efficiency and a seamless operational flow.

SouthWest Quadrant

Lekki Free Zone Development Company

www.lfzdc.org

Lekki Free Zone Development Company (LFZDC) was set up as a Joint Venture Partnership between the Lagos State Government via Lekki Worldwide Investments Limited (LWIL), and a Consortium of Chinese Investors called China Africa Lekki Investment Limited (CALIL). CALIL is (made up of China Civil Engineering Construction Corporation, China Railway Construction Company, China Africa Development Fund, and Nanjing Jiangning Economic Development Zone).

LFZDC is a registered Free Zone Company under the Nigeria Export Processing Zones Act 63 of 1992 and licensed to Develop and Manage the Southwest Quadrant of the Lekki Free Zone Project.

The Southwest Quadrant covers about 3,000 hectares of land and is currently being developed by LFZDC as a mixed-use development.

Boasting of light & heavy industries, real estate, oil & gas sector, manufacturing,etc., which were conceptualized basically to provide job opportunities for Nigerian youths, attract foreign direct investment and promote indigenous participation in industrial development which will ultimately contribute to the GDP of Lagos State.

The NorthWest Quadrant (NWQDC)

North West Quadrant Development Company

www.alarocity.com

The North West Quadrant Development Company (NWQDC) was set up as a Joint Venture Partnership between the Lagos State Government via Lekki Worldwide Investments Limited (LWIL), and the Rendevour Group, and have conceived Alaro City as a massive mixed-income, city-scale development, market-led project in the Lekki Free Zone. Formally launched in January 2019, the city has areas for offices, logistics and warehousing, homes, schools, healthcare facilities, hotels, entertainment and 150 hectares of parks and open spaces.

Authorized by both the Federal Government of Nigeria, through the Nigeria Export Processing Zones Authority, and the Lagos State Government as an entity to develop, operate, administer and manage, Alaro city and its occupants enjoy all Free Zone benefits in line with the laws of NEPZA.

Alaro City planned for 60,000 residents within low, medium and high-density housing in the city which has been designed and developed in a modular format with the first 500 hectares, master-planned by world-renowned planners Skidmore Ownings Merril in partnership with local consultants.

Rendeavour estimates that around USD250 million of bulk infrastructure will be invested within the city and at least USD1.5billion of construction by those building homes, factories, and social amenities; which will automatically translate to tens of thousands of employment opportunities during the construction phases.
